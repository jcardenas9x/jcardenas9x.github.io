Just a little over 1 week ago, I had left my job at [Hong Kong Esports Ltd.](https://hkesports.com/) after four years of working there as a full stack developer. For my initial couple of years there, I had worked on internal web applications and WordPress plugins that consumed data from a database. It basically dealt with match and player statistics in the esports scene, primarily League of Legends. Eventually, I had been shifted to work full time on a mobile and web application called [Huboo](https://www.huboo.com/tw) which aimed to bridge the gap between viewers and popular streamers and online personalities.

The application launched on the Google Play Store and Apple App Store in January of 2017, powered by MeteorJS at the time. Earlier this year we revamped the architecture of the application for the sake of performance.  I could say it went very well, with development time being cut in half compared to initial development. However, I soon found out that the company would be pivoting and essentially my role would be drastically decrease in importance. This made me leave the company as of October of this year.

I am currently in the process of transition and will most likely be employed at a different company in the near future. During the interim and with the time I have free, I planned to start a new personal blog focusing on two different topics - programming and translation - though in some cases the two can be inextricably linked.

This blog aims to talk about my personal dealings in programming as well as my observations when it comes to translation and localization, as I believe myself to be very opinionated in all of those things. 

As an aside, as I don't consider myself a very graphically inclined person, this blog is currently using somebody else's theme - it hasn't been maintained in a couple of years and I'll probably redo the entire layout sometime in the future when I get better at the whole aesthetics thing. Anyway, here's to a growth in my career as a developer and here's to a new journey for me!
